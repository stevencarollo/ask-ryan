# Loida Velasquez — Bilingual Prospecting, Expireds & FSBOs (Researched Dossier)

## Background & Credentials
- Real Broker LLC agent licensed in California AND Florida; started December 2014 from marketing/advertising, shadowed before licensing, first listing November 2015 — built entirely through cold calling, door knocking, expireds and FSBOs.
- YouTube: 95K+ subscribers, 5M+ views — "no-fluff, real-world" prospecting training; hosts the New In Real Estate Podcast; runs New Agent Bootcamp. Bilingual (EN/ES); trains Spanish-first agents in her community and serves Spanish-speaking clients in both languages.
- Growth truth she shares: it took ~3 years of weekly videos before the following "exploded" — consistency compounds late.

## Philosophy
- Expireds & FSBOs are "the lowest hanging fruit" — these owners already signaled intent to sell; most agents avoid them out of rejection fear.
- Approach "from a place of authenticity and wanting to help, rather than making money."
- Emotional control: "I can only control how I respond." Rejection is not personal data about you.
- Competitive fire: "Let me show you that I can actually be very good at this."
- "Always follow through and overcome your fear... the business can be a big emotional roller coaster, but the reward is so much greater."

## Named Frameworks & Practices
- **30 real conversations a day** — held even during rejection streaks; she credits a major deal to call #30 after a day of nos. Consistency IS the strategy.
- **Discovery before pitch**: open with "What happened?" and "Why were you trying to sell?" — understand motivation before offering anything. Key read: "The more frustrated someone is over the phone, it's probably because they were very motivated to sell." (Frustration = motivation signal, not a hang-up signal.)
- **4-5 responses per objection** — build multiple prepared answers for every common objection, drilled through relentless ROLE-PLAY with agents from other regions to absorb different personalities and communication styles.
- **Daily structure**: prospecting in the morning → follow-ups & appointments in the afternoon → family in the evening → videos in between.
- **Content doctrine**: "The best videos come from explaining the things you already know." Optimize for WATCH TIME, not views or likes. One video a week, forever.

## Script (verbatim, from REDX live-call feature)
Expired listing opener:
"Hello! Is this Rick?" → "Hi Rick! My name is Loida and I'm a local real estate agent. I'm calling about your home on 123 Main St. I noticed it recently came off the market as an expired. When will you start interviewing agents to get it sold?" → "Did you get any showings on your property?" → "So if the home sold in less than 30 days, that wouldn't be an issue for you, right?"
- Techniques inside it: assumptive interview question ("when will you start interviewing agents" — not IF), diagnostic question (showings = price vs marketing diagnosis), assumptive trial close ("wouldn't be an issue for you, right?") to surface motivation and timeline.

## Mistakes She Calls Out
- Avoiding expireds/FSBOs because of rejection fear — leaving the most motivated sellers to braver agents.
- Pitching before asking what happened.
- Quitting the daily standard when a week goes badly.
- Chasing vanity metrics (views/likes) instead of watch time and consistency.
