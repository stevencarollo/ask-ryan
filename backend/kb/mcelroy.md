# Ken McElroy — Multifamily & Apartment Investing (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Principal/co-partner of MC Companies (multifamily investment + management); 26+ years senior-level multifamily asset/property management and development experience; Robert Kiyosaki's **Rich Dad Advisor** for real estate; author of "The ABCs of Real Estate Investing," "The Advanced Guide to Real Estate Investing," and "ABCs of Property Management"; large YouTube/podcast education presence; cited as purchasing $400M+ of real estate in a recent two-year span.

## Core Thesis (verified)
- **"Successful investing isn't just about buying the right property — it's about managing it effectively."** Operations ARE the investment; the deal is made at the buy, but the return is made in management.
- Buy for CASH FLOW, not appreciation — appreciation is the bonus, cash flow is the business. Multifamily advantages he teaches: economies of scale, reliable cash flow, and **forced appreciation through management**.

## Valuation & Deal Math (his teaching sequence)
- Value = NOI ÷ cap rate — determine NOI and cap rates to value any property; then compute loan payments from financing terms and **profit = NOI − debt service**, giving cash-on-cash return. Because value = NOI ÷ cap, every $1 of added NOI at a 5-cap creates ~$20 of value — the whole value-add playbook hangs on this arithmetic.
- Underwriting doctrine (his most-repeated warning): buy on ACTUAL trailing numbers, never the seller's proforma — the proforma is a sales brochure. "Negotiate the deal based on the numbers."

## Management Value-Add Levers (verified three + classics)
1. **Tenant selection**: qualified tenants reduce turnover, evictions, and vacancy — the silent profit killers.
2. **Rent optimization**: market research to find the rent that maximizes revenue while minimizing vacancy — not the highest rent, the RIGHT rent.
3. **Expense reduction**: negotiate vendor rates, streamline operations.
- Plus his classic income adds: utility bill-back (RUBS), laundry/parking/storage/pet income, loss-to-lease capture.

## Myths He Debunks (book's opening premise)
- "You need money to make money," "real estate is passive," "you can't find deals in hot markets" — the "secrets of finding hidden profits most investors miss" are in operations and underwriting discipline, not timing genius.

## Market & Debt Doctrine
- Path-of-progress submarkets: jobs + population + income growth; avoid single-employer towns. Long-term fixed debt; refinance to return capital rather than selling the golden goose; cash flow means never being a forced seller.

## Example Questions He Uniquely Answers
1. "Value this 12-unit from its actual T-12 numbers."
2. "Which line items add NOI fastest after closing?"
3. "The seller's proforma says 7% — what do I actually underwrite?"
4. "What does professional-grade tenant screening look like?"
5. "Raise rents or fill vacancies — which first?"
6. "When do I refi vs sell a stabilized property?"
