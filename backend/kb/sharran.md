# Sharran Srivatsaa — Business Strategy, Luxury & Wealth (Researched Dossier)

- Former Goldman Sachs and Credit Suisse banker; scaled **Teles Properties 10x in 5 years to $3.4B in sales**, sold to Douglas Elliman; **President of Real (NASDAQ: REAX)** as it became the fastest-growing publicly traded brokerage; **now CEO/Managing Partner at Acquisition.com** with Alex & Leila Hormozi (still on Real's board + ARC Multifamily Group). 4x Inc. 500 entrepreneur, 5 exits in 19 years, two billion-dollar companies built.
- Platforms: the **5AM Club** call for entrepreneurs (global morning-discipline movement), **Business School Podcast** ("simple, tactical playbooks for founders and operators"), "My Next Billion" Substack. Featured in Forbes, CNBC, Success.

## Philosophy
- Everything is a named, teachable, repeatable playbook — "simple, tactical playbooks" is the brand promise. If a process can't be named and taught, it can't scale.
- CEO-of-your-calendar: morning discipline (5AM Club), theme days, energy management over time management.
- Wealth stack: agent P&L vs OWNER P&L — build recurring assets (teams, revenue share, equity, investments) alongside commissions; his own path (banker → operator → owner → investor) is the teaching model.

## Listing & Luxury Craft
- His listing-consultation structure claims a **94.5% success rate "in the living room" with clients never met before** — built from "battle-tested listing presentation skills across hundreds of appointments, across marketplaces, across agents, and across price points." Structure over charisma: diagnose before prescribing, let the client conclude the price.
- Companion assets he distributes: Buyer Consultation Process, Seller/Listing Process, Listing Pre-Launch Blueprint — pre-launch engineering (the marketing begins before the listing goes live).
- Luxury positioning doctrine: become the market-data authority — publish the numbers others quote; positioning beats prospecting at the high end.

## Deal & Negotiation Architecture
- Every negotiation has levers beyond price: timing, terms, certainty, convenience, ego — trade what's cheap-to-you for what's valuable-to-them.
- Referral engineering: give referrals FIRST and track them like revenue.

## What He Argues Against
- Unnamed, improvised processes (unteachable = unscalable).
- Commission-only thinking with no owner P&L.
- Charisma-dependent listing presentations (structure produces the 94.5%, not personality).
- Busy-ness as a badge — energy and leverage over hours.
