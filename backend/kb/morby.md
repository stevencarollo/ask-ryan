# Pace Morby — Creative Finance & Subject-To (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Host of A&E's "Triple Digit Flip"; author of "Wealth without Cash" (BiggerPockets); built a portfolio of **1,000+ doors / ~$150M in assets** heavily via creative finance; founder of the SubTo community/education brand; built the brand by documenting deals authentically on social ("visible action rather than claims").

## Core Philosophy (verbatim)
- **"You have to either get better or get creative."** When you can't win on price or credit, win on STRUCTURE.
- Toolkit doctrine: carry MANY tools (wholesale, direct purchase, MLS, sub-to, seller finance, hybrid, novation) and match the tool to the SELLER'S problem — never one method applied uniformly.

## The Structures (his teaching)
- **Subject-to (Subto)**: buyer takes title (name on deed) while the seller's existing mortgage stays in place and buyer makes the payments. The loan is NOT formally assumed — the debt stays in the seller's name. Best when the seller needs debt relief + speed. Handle the due-on-sale risk honestly and hold reserves.
- **Seller finance**: the seller becomes the bank — the play for free-and-clear owners. His signature negotiation: price OR terms, never both. Verified example: bought a $130,000 property with **$0 down and 0% interest at $350/month, renting for $1,600/month** — full price for perfect terms.
- **Hybrid (sub-to + seller carry)**: when the seller has equity ON TOP of existing debt — take over the loan sub-to AND the seller carries the equity as a note.
- **Novation**: partner with the seller — renovate/list on their title, split upside above a guaranteed floor.
- Buyer/seller win logic he teaches: seller nets more (full price, no repairs, no showings); buyer skips banks, appraisals, and bureaucracy.

## Numbers (verified)
- Converts ~**1 in every 22 leads** — "double and even triple the rate" of other buyers in his market, because creative offers say yes where cash offers can't.
- Operation volume: ~10-12 wholesale deals + 10-12 creative-finance deals per month.

## Deal Sourcing (his channels)
- Text campaigns, wholesalers and agents (his top intro source — network via Facebook groups and Meetups), door knocking, probate attorneys. Other investors' DEAD leads (no equity for cash offers) are creative-finance gold.

## Seller Conversation Doctrine
- The biggest hurdle is psychological: "the thought of having to call sellers and talk to them" — he teaches by demonstrating LIVE seller calls. Storytelling converts: explain structures through real deal narratives (the $350/month story), not jargon. Dig for the REAL problem (payment burden, tenant nightmare, probate mess) — the structure falls out of the problem.
- Paperwork discipline (his standard): close through a title company/attorney, seller gets independent counsel offered, third-party loan servicing collects payments — protects both sides.

## What He Argues Against
- "You need cash and credit to invest" (the whole brand exists to disprove it).
- One-tool operators bidding price against cash buyers.
- Hiding the ball from sellers — the structures work explained honestly.
- Networking laziness: "use connections and network whenever and however you can."
