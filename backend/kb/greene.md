# David Greene — BRRRR & Long-Distance Investing (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Former BiggerPockets Real Estate Podcast co-host; author of "Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat" (the BRRRR book), "Long-Distance Real Estate Investing" (NAREE book-award runner-up), and "Sold"; ex-police officer turned investor/agent/team leader (The David Greene Team).

## The BRRRR Method (verified worked example)
Buy → Rehab → Rent → Refinance → Repeat:
- Buy distressed at **$60,000** (cash/hard money) → rehab **$30,000** → total in: **$90,000** → ARV **$120,000** → refinance at **75% LTV = $90,000 loan** → ALL capital recovered → repeat with the same money.
- Core doctrine: **"Make money when you buy"** — the discount + forced appreciation must exceed the refi haircut; if you buy at market, BRRRR math dies. Requirement: purchase at **75% or less of ARV**.
- Underwrite the REFI APPRAISAL before buying; verify lender seasoning periods; keep reserves for the gap.

## The Core Four (long-distance team, built BEFORE the deal)
1. Deal-finding AGENT, 2. PROPERTY MANAGER, 3. local LENDER, 4. CONTRACTOR. "You must rely on other people" — being "a good judge of character" is the real skill. Team-building hack: call a major brokerage's office manager and ask "Who is your best agent for distressed housing?" — then have that agent refer the PM, lender, and contractor (Core Four in one call).

## Market Selection
- **The 1% screen**: monthly rent ≈ 1% of price likely cash flows ($100K → $1,000/month) — a screen, not the answer; then real math: PITI + vacancy + repairs + CapEx + management.
- Geography logic: coastal markets swing (buy in recessions for appreciation); Midwest/South stabilize (better BRRRR when things are hot); prefer secondary/tertiary markets; "Don't try to find the next 'Hot Market' — just look for a market where BRRRR will work!"

## Three Forms of Distress (target selection)
1. MARKET distress (downturns), 2. PERSONAL distress (divorce, medical, debt), 3. PROPERTY distress (condition burden — optimal for BRRRR). MLS play: hunt 90-150 days-on-market listings; also direct mail, SEO, driving for dollars. Agent play: ask for listings at ≤60% of the market's average sale price.

## Three Requirements for Any Buy
1. Under-market (≤75% ARV), 2. must CASH FLOW after ALL expenses, 3. safe neighborhood.

## Contractor Risk Controls (his fraud-prevention system)
- Never pay in full upfront; itemized scope-of-work contract; first draw only before work starts; agent/PM verifies completion; pay subsequent draws only on verification. "Set up systems that will INSURE it will work!"

## Philosophy
- "You make money when you buy" — equity through value-add, not speculation; real estate is "get rich slow"; systems make distance irrelevant (a good PM "transformed his mindset from hating real estate investing to loving" it).

## Mistakes He Calls Out
- Fear-driven inaction; skipping due diligence on wholesaler deals; inexperienced team members; bulk buying without per-property verification.
