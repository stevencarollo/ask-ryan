# Carlos Reyes — Investing & Wholesaling en Español (Researched Dossier)

## Story & Credentials
- Born into extreme poverty in Sonora, Mexico (no running water/electricity); came to the U.S. undocumented as a boy; raised by a single mother working two jobs at $7-8.25/hour for 3 kids — the origin of his relentless work ethic: he "attributes his hustle, perseverance, and inability to quit to observing her dedication."
- 14 years in corporate America from age 17 (youngest retail branch manager at 19 — where he learned personnel management). Left in 2013; with partner Sal Shakir built National Cash Offer / OfferKey into a national wholesaling operation: **$200M+ in wholesaled real estate, 8-figure annual earnings**, deals across 12-15 markets simultaneously; 31+ businesses, equity in 17 private companies. "All In Nation" education/community brand; ALL IN podcast.

## Core Philosophy (verbatim)
- **"We're not real estate investors that do marketing. We're marketers that do real estate investing."** — the single most important frame he teaches: master marketing and the asset class is interchangeable.
- Numbers religion: "We're about the numbers. I can tell you how much we've spent into a particular market and what marketing avenue and how much money we got back as far as ROI."
- The immigrant mindset as fuel: nothing was given; use having-nothing as the reason you can't quit ("How To Use Nothing To Inspire You To Gain Everything").

## Named Frameworks
- **Data-driven market selection** (virtual wholesaling): score markets on cash-transaction volume, average days on market, median sale price, and demographics before spending a dollar; he later hired a data scientist to sharpen targeting. First virtual market: Las Vegas — first deal $5,000 via bandit signs + mail.
- **Channel-ranked marketing engine** (his current ranking): (1) COLD CALLING with vetted high-level callers, (2) PPC, (3) direct mail (ROI compressed from 10x to 8x but still viable; postcard formats). Every channel tracked per-market with ROI KPIs.
- **Real budget arc**: started 2016 at $500/month marketing; scaled to ~$97,350/month ($876K YTD by Q3) at a 5x ROI (7x is the target). Teaches that scale = spend discipline + tracking, not luck.
- **7-figure structure rule**: a CFO is MANDATORY past 7 figures; data scientist next; then hiring processes, pay structures, conversion training. Systems built on CRM automation (Podio-based InvestorAutomation).
- **Mastermind model**: intensive small-room events (100-150 max, ~20 hours of strategy) over stadium seminars; 6-month mentee programs covering branding, marketing, systems, acquisitions, scaling.

## Community & Bilingual Angle
- His story IS the marketing to the Latino community: undocumented immigrant → $200M in deals — patrimonio (generational wealth) through ownership, taught in both languages across his podcast/content. The credibility mechanism: show the journey and the receipts, not the lifestyle.
- Teaches Spanish-speaking entrepreneurs that the community's trust networks (family referral chains, WhatsApp groups, Spanish radio/media) are an untapped acquisition channel most English-only operators never reach.

## What He Argues Against
- Being an "investor who dabbles in marketing" (backwards — see core quote).
- Scaling without a CFO or KPI discipline ("if you can't state your ROI per channel per market, you're gambling").
- Single-market dependence — the system should be portable to any market the data likes.
- Waiting for perfect conditions; his first deal came from bandit signs and $500/month.
