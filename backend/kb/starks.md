## Named Frameworks
- **The specialized listing protocol** — a divorce-specific listing system that "anticipates, prevents, and mitigates conflict" while maximizing value: equal simultaneous communication to both parties (and counsel), documented everything, decisions pre-wired in writing (price changes, showing terms, response deadlines) so nothing becomes a new fight.
- **The audition principle** (attorney referrals): "It's an audition, right? You prove yourself, you prove how good you are... what kind of aspirin you are to their headache." One well-run court case converts an entire firm into a referral source.
- **Referral timing rule**: do NOT solicit attorney referrals during an active case — perform first, then schedule the relationship lunch after the case closes. Work BOTH sides: opposing counsel on today's case is tomorrow's referral source.
- **Court leverage tools**: when a spouse refuses to sign, obtain court orders appointing an ELISOR (court-authorized signatory) to execute paperwork for the non-compliant party — requires documented proof of non-cooperation attempts and sometimes testifying. Knowing this path exists changes how you handle obstruction: document, don't argue.
- **The mini-market concept**: divorce real estate is its own ecosystem — court timelines, stipulations and orders govern the sale (exclusive use, who approves price cuts, proceeds distribution), layered with emotional and financial pressure. Standard listing practice fails here.

## Complications She Trains Agents to Handle
- Restraining orders that prevent open houses or lockboxes; substance abuse, incarceration, personality disorders; delinquent mortgages and foreclosure clocks running DURING the divorce; title encumbrances (IRS liens, judgments) surfacing mid-case; the in-spouse blocking showings (get showing terms INTO the order).
- Exposure management: agents hear abuse disclosures and trauma narratives — professionalism means empathy without absorption or advocacy.

## What She Argues Against
- Taking sides (the fatal error — it kills the deal, the referral source, and possibly triggers testimony problems).
- Treating divorce listings like standard listings with sad clients.
- Chasing attorney referrals with marketing instead of earning them with courtroom-grade competence.
- Winging high-conflict situations without documentation.
