## The AMPLIFII Framework (core system, from his book)
Three parts, in order — "It's the SEQUENCE that counts":
1. **FRAME** — "Always frame your conversation so people are prepared." The frame is set BEFORE the content; whoever sets the frame controls how the message lands (price frame vs value frame vs risk frame).
2. **MESSAGE** — "Deliver your message clearly and concisely."
3. **TIE-DOWN** — "Add a tie-down that locks in your message" — typically a question that prompts agreement and cements the point (e.g., after a quality message: a question that makes them affirm quality matters).
- Book example he uses: Alan Shepard's 1961 launch — frame (historic moment, world watching), message (the "lowest bidder" safety quip), tie-down (agreement question on quality).

## The Origin / Identity Story
- The origin story is "a powerful frame" answering exactly two questions: **how you arrived at where you are today** and **why you love serving the customers you serve.** Every agent needs this told in ~90 seconds at every listing appointment BEFORE the CMA — it transfers trust before numbers.
- "Credibility from obstacles makes people listen and see your value" — the struggle is the credential; package the story in the right sequence or the audience misreads you (his "story packaging" doctrine).

## Neuroscience & Influence Doctrine
- "Influence is about understanding how our brains and those of our audience process information."
- "Influence is not about sounding good or being a polished speaker. It's about getting people to trust you and act on your ideas."
- "There are no victims in good communication. Influence isn't power or control, it's about building trust and communicating value."
- **Nervous-system reframe**: nerves aren't a malfunction — reframe the adrenaline as a sharpening tool before presentations/listing appointments.
- **Audience intelligence**: know and survey the audience BEFORE presenting; the same message needs different frames for different rooms.
- **Belief mastery**: beliefs/values/memories can be examined and consciously rewritten — where did your money beliefs and fear-of-rejection beliefs come from? (Applies directly to call reluctance.)
- Recommends pairing with Oren Klaff's "Pitch Anything" (frame control) — same intellectual lineage.

## Applied to Real Estate (his keynote material)
- Listing presentations: set the frame first (what this meeting is and isn't), identity story second, THEN data. Never open with the CMA.
- Objections are frames to reframe, not questions to answer: "expensive compared to what — the cost of selling twice?"
- "Controlling a room" craft: primacy/recency (open and close strong — never end on price), pause as power, deliberate movement anchors.
- Bilingual advantage: emotional resonance doubles in the client's heart language — deliver numbers in whichever language is precise, but tell the STORY in Spanish for Spanish-dominant clients.
