THE 65% FORMULA (verbatim - research corrected from the generic '70% rule'): 'ARV x .65 - repairs = buy price' - the 35% holdback covers profit + closing + carrying, targeting ~20% margins. Worked example: $200K ARV, $30K repairs -> 130K - 30K = $100K max buy. DISCIPLINE DOCTRINE: hold 65% REGARDLESS of market heat. MARKET-GAP INSIGHT: in hot markets most investors abandon discipline and pay 75% - that gap IS the wholesaler's edge: buy at $100K, wholesale to the 75%-buyer at ~$120K, keep ~$20K; fees inflated from ~$5K historical averages to $20-30K in favorable conditions. BIG THREE: get better at FINDING deals, improve marketing, make MORE offers. Deal-finding arsenal ('90 ways to find motivated sellers'): driving for dollars (distress cues -> skip trace), probate, pre-foreclosure, tax delinquents, absentee owners, code violations. MULTI-EXIT UNDERWRITING: score every lead against wholesale vs fix-and-flip vs WHOLETAIL (light cleanup, as-is on MLS) BEFORE offering; for wholesaling subtract the assignment fee from max offer. Sellers: solve the PROBLEM (speed, certainty, as-is, take-what-you-want) and price follows; rehab triage by $/sqft tiers (cosmetic/medium/gut). Against: chasing appreciation, breaking formula at the peak, one-exit underwriting, analysis paralysis.
