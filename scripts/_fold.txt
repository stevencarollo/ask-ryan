'Cap rate is a snapshot, not a return.' Underwrite the lease, not just the building. Actuals over proforma.
RESEARCHED (tylercauble.com cap-rate guide w/ verbatim quotes + 2026 ranges; NNN explainer; investing guides). Nashville boutique CRE broker (Cauble Group), CEO Hamilton Development (2.1M+ sq ft since 2019), author 'Open for Business'; courses built for INVESTORS/principals. CAP RATE: NOI / value - 'a $1,000,000 building generating $80,000 in NOI is an 8% cap'; 'cap rate is a SNAPSHOT, not a return on investment'; 'a 7-cap industrial deal and a 7-cap retail deal aren't the same thing'; pair with cash-on-cash and IRR ('one of three or four metrics... not the most important one'). HIS 2026 RANGES: MF Class A 4.5-6%, industrial big-box 5.5-7%, CBD Class A office 6-8% (post-WFH repricing), Class B office 8.5-11%, anchored retail 6.5-8%, investment-grade net lease 5-6.5%, retail strips 7-9%. Reading bands: 5 = trophy, 6 = solid Class A growth-market, 7 = stabilized market rate, 8+ = risk OR value-add needing active management. LEASE LITERACY: lease type changes the ECONOMICS - NNN (tenant pays taxes/insurance/CAM; 'backbone of single-tenant retail... sold as mailbox money'), modified gross, full-service gross; underwrite the LEASE. FOUR MISTAKES (verbatim list): proforma over trailing-12 ACTUALS; cross-asset cap comparison ignoring credit/lease-length/capex; ignoring NOI growth trajectory; confusing cap rate (unleveraged) with cash-on-cash (leveraged). SMALL-CRE DOCTRINE: start neighborhood-scale (strips, mixed-use) below institutional radar; value-add = rents to market at renewal, lease-up, NNN conversion, expense recapture; learn commercial DEBT before the deal.
