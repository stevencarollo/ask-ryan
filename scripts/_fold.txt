No prequalification, no appointment. The 7 P's: Previewing, Prequalifying, Preparing, the PLP, and a Polished Professional Presentation.
RESEARCHED (timandjulieharris.com training posts + prequalification scripts post; Inman 'Best Coaches'). Decades of coaching, daily podcast, books incl. 'The Real Estate Treasure Map.' DOCTRINE: listings are the business - the machine is 'locate & prequalify motivated sellers' then Listing Evaluation -> Pre-Listing Package -> Presentation to 'get the listing every single time.' THE 7 P's (verbatim): 'The profit comes from Previewing, Prequalifying, Preparing, the PLP, and a Polished, Professional Presentation.' 7-step listing machine: generate lead -> follow up -> prequalify -> preview -> prepare/PLP -> present -> sign; skipping prequalification is the cardinal sin. Lead-gen rule: MINIMUM 5 'spokes' running simultaneously; 'do more prospecting than you think necessary.' BUYER PREQUAL SCRIPT (verbatim, in order): (1) 'I assume because you're calling me directly, you're not already working with another agent?' (2) 'What price has your lender told you NOT to go above?' (one question surfaces cash/pre-approved/no-lender), (3) 'Which home in the neighborhood do you plan on selling?' - 'many buyer leads are listing leads in disguise,' (4) 'Paint me a picture - ideally, what are you looking for in your next home?' (5) 'On a scale of 1-10, where would you rate your desire to write up an offer?' + 'What would it take to make you a 10?' SELLER PREQUAL framework: motivation (why/where/when), timeline, price expectation, all decision-makers present, competition - and the PLP arrives BEFORE the appointment so the presentation closes instead of educates. Treasure Map planning: income goal reverse-engineered to transactions -> appointments -> contacts -> daily discipline. Against: unprequalified appointments, buyer-first models, one-pillar lead gen.
