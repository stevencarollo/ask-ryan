Invest directly with the government. 'I won't buy anything unless I look at it.' Know the rules, do the homework.
RESEARCHED (REtipster ep196 long-form; tedthomas.com first-tax-deed guide; AdvantaIRA ep121). 30+ years as the tax lien/deed authority. LIENS: 'a tax lien is nothing more than a piece of paper' - buy the delinquent tax debt from the county, get paid principal + statutory interest at redemption (~97% pay off; unredeemed -> foreclose, usually mortgage-free). State rates he cites: AZ 16%, FL 18%, IL 18%, IA 24%, TX up to 25%; range 8-50% - every county's code differs. DEEDS: county auctions the title - 'mortgage-free real estate for 10, 20 or 30 cents on the dollar' (CA = deed state, 5+ yrs delinquent). THE 20-30-3-1 FUNNEL: research 20-30 properties, bid ~3, win 1-2; max bid from exit math BEFORE arriving; walk away freely. Due-diligence stack: list -> assessed vs starting bid -> GIS/satellite -> PHYSICAL look ('decent house in that neighborhood... not the best house in the ghetto') -> comps -> surviving-lien check (IRS windows) -> exit documented. Bid-thinning: at $50K half the room exits, $100K 90%, $125K 95% - higher targets = fewer bidders; in-room beats online ('in the room, people push the price down'). Mechanics: cashier's checks; deposits cap bidding power (Broward: max bid = 20x deposit); payment windows immediate to 15+ days. Post-auction: SECURE the property first; quiet title $2-5K / 2-18 months before insured resale - EXCEPT seller-finance exits (you keep title): 'People will pay you MORE than asking if you'll finance it' (8-11%). Target $25-50K profit/deal (example: $58K all-in -> $162K). Start with $20-25K+ capital; under $5K = liens only. All failure = 'not knowing the rules' + 'not doing your homework.' Agent angle: tax-delinquent LISTS are motivated sellers YEARS before auction; excess proceeds belong to the former owner (service niche).
