RESEARCHED (Homes & Gardens 4-tips + symmetry + designed-to-age features; studio-mcgee.com; aesthetic analyses). Co-founder of Studio McGee / McGee & Co.; Netflix's Dream Home Makeover; Target Threshold lines; mission (verbatim): 'Make Life Beautiful for ourselves and those around us.' PHILOSOPHY: homes 'both beautiful and deeply personal... not a showcase of trends but a reflection of the people who live there'; 'a good set of core design values can be applied to any home, even with the smallest of footprints.' DESIGN RULES: SCALE AND PROPORTION FIRST - the most common failure; size UP (rugs that anchor the furniture, art/lighting proportionate) - correct scale is what makes rooms read 'designed.' LAYER TEXTURES FOR TIMELESSNESS: neutral palettes need texture depth - wood, metal, fabrics for 'a rich, tactile experience'; 'layering materials creates a timeless scheme that feels curated and cozy.' BREAK THE SYMMETRY: 'a delicate balance between symmetry, asymmetry, and finally ditching matching sets' - perfect symmetry reads flat. DESIGN TO AGE: her Arizona new-build was designed 'to look better in 20 years than today' - write history INTO new houses (patina materials, vintage anchors). THE 4 SMALL-SPACE TIPS (verified): (1) furniture with subtle details over statement pieces, (2) vintage accents ALWAYS, (3) layer up the textures, (4) a romantic flourish = laid-back luxury. Agent application: budget listing prep = scale-check -> layer textiles -> one vintage anchor per key room -> styled-but-lived-in photos; sell the TRANSFORMATION delta. Against: matching sets (#1 amateur tell), undersized rugs/art, trend-chasing, flat single-texture neutrals.
