RESEARCHED (Forbes profile; imgnyc.com; Love Happens feature). Founder (2007) of Interior Marketing Group - NYC's dominant luxury staging firm: 100 employees, ~200 properties/year, $10M penthouses to $100M estates; clients incl. Bezos and Kardashian; seen in Owning Manhattan/MDLNY/Succession. RESULT PROOF: $49M + $48.5M Palm Beach estates, $35M South Beach condo sold FULLY FURNISHED, $62.8M Hollywood Hills (market's largest sale that year); 'most projects sell fully furnished.' ORIGIN: dot-com collapse -> broke on mom's sofa ('never rely on just one revenue stream') -> as a frustrated broker saw luxury listings languish: buyers 'weren't just shopping for square footage - they wanted a FANTASY, a future... no one was staging high-end properties with intention'; first intuitive staging sold fast, full ask, cash. PHILOSOPHY (verbatim): 'It's never just about filling a space - it's about creating an emotional connection, a story that makes buyers fall in love'; brand promise: 'magazine-worthy design in record time.' Staging = MERCHANDISING a price point - stage to the TARGET buyer's aspirational life, never the seller's taste. METHOD: the MOMENTS method - photographable aspirational vignettes (champagne terrace, styled vanity, breakfast moment) - buyers buy the moment, photos sell the click; vacant luxury reads cold and SMALL - furniture proves scale; every room gets a JOB (dead space renamed office/gym/gallery); neutral-luxe + oversized art/mirrors; declutter -> depersonalize -> ADD BACK curated layers (empty isn't staged); sell-it-furnished flywheel. ROI frame for sellers: staging cost vs the FIRST PRICE CUT - better photos, smaller list-to-sale gap, fewer days on market.
