Mirror, label, calibrated question. Negotiation begins at 'no'. Never split the difference - trade.
RESEARCHED (Graham Mann full book notes; Rev Real Estate School ep91 agent application; famvestor cheat sheet). Ex-FBI lead kidnapping negotiator; Never Split the Difference; Black Swan Group. TACTICS: MIRROR - repeat last 1-3 words as a question, then silence (intention: 'please, help me understand'); LABEL - 'It seems like / It sounds like' (never 'I'm hearing'), then SILENCE; ACCUSATION AUDIT - say every negative they could think about you FIRST ('taking the sting out'); CALIBRATED QUESTIONS - how/what that make THEM solve it: 'How am I supposed to do that?', 'What about this is important to you?', 'How will we know we're on track?'; get to 'THAT'S right' (breakthrough) - 'you're right' is a brush-off; NO-ORIENTED questions - 'no' feels safe, negotiation BEGINS at no ('Is now a bad time to talk?'); RULE OF THREE - same agreement three ways in one conversation. ACKERMAN MODEL: open at 65% of target -> 85% -> 95% -> 100% in decreasing increments; precise NON-ROUND numbers ($37,263 reads calculated); attach a small non-cash item at the end; let them anchor first (usually). CRAFT: late-night FM DJ voice for tension; 7-38-55 - label tone/word incongruence; pronoun analysis - liars go third-person, real DECIDERS use few 'I' statements; the FAIR technique - 'I want you to feel like you are being treated fairly at all times.' BENDING REALITY: loss framing beats gain framing; emotions anchored before numbers. BLACK SWANS: 3+ unknown unknowns per deal - dig into their 'religion', unguarded face time at session edges. Principles: slow down; 'no deal is better than a bad deal'; yes without an implementation HOW is meaningless. RE sequence for any hard talk: mirror -> label -> calibrated question.
