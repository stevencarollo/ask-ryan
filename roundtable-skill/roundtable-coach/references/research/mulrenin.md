# Brandon Mulrenin — Reverse Selling, FSBO & Expired Prospecting (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Michigan broker, CEO of Brookstone Realtors; founder/CEO of reverseselling.com coaching; author of "Reverse Selling: How Real Estate Agents Can Turn Cold Calls Into Clients"; sold 116 homes his FIRST year as an agent; hosts The Reverse Selling Podcast ("where high pressure selling is extinct"). One featured student: 250 homes/$1M+ commissions in year 6 without a large team.

## Core Philosophy
- Reverse Selling = communicate with FSBOs "in a way that ALIGNS with what they believe — that they don't need a listing agent." Never fight the frame; join it, then let questions do the work.
- Sell without being salesy: valued advisor, not pursuer. Detach from the outcome so the prospect sells YOU.
- Disqualification doctrine: gently push prospects AWAY — "properly disqualifying prospects... so that the prospect actually invites you over to interview to take the listing."

## Named Frameworks (from the book)
- **The ASP Method** (objection handling, 3 stages): (1) **Acknowledge** — validate their position: "I see where you're coming from" / "Given today's market, you'll likely succeed selling independently"; (2) **Strategic empathy** — communicate their perspective back, which "disarms them, opens them up to listening"; (3) **Pivot** — immediately transition to a fresh relevant question WITHOUT pausing.
- **Socratic questioning** — never give the conclusion; ask questions that make them generate it: "What risks face us if your house stays listed long-term?" People embrace ideas they generate themselves.
- **The Prospecting Waterfall** (lead classification): Appointments (the goal) → Hot Leads (real interest + consent to follow up) → Warm Contacts (nurture) → Seller Nurtures (long-term future need). Every call ends with the lead placed on the waterfall — nothing is a dead end.
- **The Big Three hand-raisers**: Expired listings, FSBOs, and FRBOs (for-rent-by-owner — the overlooked, least competitive segment). All three already signaled intent.
- **The Seed Plant** (FSBO visit closer): end the first home visit telling the seller they're in GOOD shape and will likely sell on their own — "call me if you run into issues." The anti-pitch that wins the callback when reality hits.
- **Non-threatening language**: swap hard words for soft ones — "usually," "possibly," "contemplate," "sensible." Not "What time works to meet?" but "Would you be open to coffee one morning, and which days usually work best?"
- **Empowerment tactics**: hand control back explicitly — "We won't make agreements for now" / "It's your choice whether collaborating makes sense." FSBOs are pressure-sensitized; removing pressure removes resistance.

## Tonality (his signature emphasis)
- Downswing endings, slow pace, zero "commission breath." The disqualifying frame: "I'm not sure I can even help you yet — can I ask a few questions?" flips the power dynamic. Tonality matters more than the words.

## Follow-Up Doctrine
- "Follow-up is where the fortune is made." Pipeline maturity: leads ripen over months or YEARS. The agent who's still calling in month 9 wins the listing the month-1 crowd burned.

## Referral Network
- Build systematic relationships with professionals who meet your sellers first: divorce and probate attorneys, CPAs, financial planners, lenders, assisted-living managers — regular meetings, shared resources, proactive referrals BOTH ways.

## What He Argues Against
- Pitching, educating, or "value dumping" on the phone — the appointment is the product.
- High-pressure closes and objection "rebuttals" (ASP replaces them).
- Treating no-answer or not-now as dead (waterfall placement instead).
- Sounding like every other agent: if the FSBO expects a pitch, the absence of a pitch IS the differentiation.

## Example Questions He Uniquely Answers
1. "Give me the FSBO first-call script that doesn't trigger resistance."
2. "The expired seller says they're relisting with the same agent — ASP it."
3. "What exactly is the seed plant and when do I use it?"
4. "How do I sound — tonality — so I don't have commission breath?"
5. "What do I do with the lead who isn't ready?" (Waterfall.)
6. "Are FRBOs worth calling?" (Least competitive of the Big Three.)
