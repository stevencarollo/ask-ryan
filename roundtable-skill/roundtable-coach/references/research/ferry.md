## The Business Plan Method (verified, 5 steps)
1. **Comprehensive operating system** — define what you want, why, when each milestone lands, and how you'll execute: "You need to cover all the bases if you're going to set the right goals."
2. **The Appointments Math** — reverse-engineer the income target: GCI goal → units needed → apply YOUR conversion rates → weekly/daily appointments required → daily conversation targets. Verbatim principles: **"Math makes action obvious"** and **"No math = no focus."**
3. **Select 3-5 LISTING pillars and calendar them** — e.g., geo farm, open houses, database, content/SEO, agent-to-agent referrals. Each pillar gets scripts, KPIs, and a real budget. Warning: avoid "pillar sprawl" — dabbling across many channels weakens all of them.
4. **Systematize the client journey** — documented SOPs for speed-to-lead, listing launch, active-buyer workflow; automate CRM reminders; monthly reviews. "Without systems, your pipeline leaks."
5. **Weekly accountability** — 30-minute weekly check-in (coach or partner): review scorecards, declare the "Big 3" commitments, course-correct. Planning is a weekly cadence, not an annual event.
- Meta-rules: keep the plan to ONE PAGE; "You don't need a perfect plan — you need a plan you'll use."

## Script & Practice Doctrine
- Public script library covers expireds, FSBOs, database/sphere calls, open house, objection handling, appointment closes — his guidance: use dialogues LOOSELY, adapt to your voice, and call your database 4-6 times per year minimum. Daily role-play until tonality is natural — scripts are training wheels for conversations, not word prisons. (His full Agent Script Book is proprietary — pointed agents to the official free-resources page.)

## Marketing & Content Doctrine
- Consistency beats production value; answer the questions consumers actually ask; video is the trust accelerant. The agent as local economist-of-choice: publish the numbers, be the source.

## Accountability Culture

## What He Argues Against
- Goals without math ("no math = no focus"); pillar sprawl; annual-binge planning; unscripted winging of appointments; treating coaching/accountability as optional at scale.
