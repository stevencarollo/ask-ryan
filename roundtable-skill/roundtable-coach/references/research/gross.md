# Bill Gross — California Probate Real Estate (Researched Dossier)

## Philosophy
- Probate is a SERVICE business on a 2-year runway: "your marketing plan where your average prospect is two years from now." Plant seeds now for steady business later; average prospect converts ~2 years out.
- "If we come from being of service, you're going to accumulate people who appreciate you."
- "All you need is one strategy when you take the time to create a solid plan." One niche, done deeply, beats scattered lead-gen: once positioned as THE probate expert, "consumers assume I can sell their house" — specialization grows total volume, it doesn't shrink it.
- Positions primarily as broker-agent representing the estate for highest value (not as a lowball investor). Fee = commission at close, accepting "I won't get paid for 2 years or I never get paid."

## Named Frameworks
- **"Stuck probate" targeting** — his core play: cases already sitting in court for a year-plus. "After a year and a half of struggling... they realize you can't sell the house without me." The listing goes to whoever can un-stick the process.
- **"11 ways" multi-angle attack** — approach the SAME probate case from many angles (PR, heirs, attorney, courthouse, mail, video, referral partners). "One plus one if you work together as three, not two."
- **"Two bites at the apple"** — partner with an investor, share a VA and marketing costs, and mail the same list from both the agent angle and the investor angle simultaneously.
- **"Brand Is You"** (via his coach Don Hobbs) — pick the marketing channel matching your personal assets. Gross leveraged "gray hair" and formal presence → courthouse visits + attorney lunches instead of mass mail.
- **Probate Courthouse Challenge** — physically attend probate court for several days; "most of the action kind of happens from 8:30 till about 10 o'clock." You see the competition, the process, and live opportunities.
- **Weekly-contact standard** — minimum weekly seller contact (phone/text/email) on case status during the long process; expectation management IS the service.

## Scripts & Language
- Attorney lunch script: "I was talking to Joe Smith, his attorney. He mentioned that you work in estate planning... can we meet for lunch?" — nearly all attorneys accept lunch meetings.
- Disclosure discipline: "When you're an agent, you have to be clear upfront if you're acting as agent or acting as a principal."
- On single-touch mail: "I mailed out... I pissed everybody. Yeah, that doesn't work." Direct mail only works at tens-of-thousands-per-month scale with multi-year persistence.

## Numbers & Formulas
- Production proof: 42 transaction sides / 30 sales in 12 months in LA, built from zero leads starting 2019 (~22 of 32 listings seller-side; rest buyer-side for investors).
- **Court-confirmation overbid (CA Probate Code §10311)**: first minimum overbid = accepted offer + 10% of first $10,000 + 5% of the remainder. Example: $500,000 accepted → $525,500 minimum overbid. After the first overbid, the court sets increments (e.g., $500). Cashier's check required in the courtroom.
- IAEA authority is the fork in the road: FULL authority → PR can sell without court confirmation (Notice of Proposed Action to heirs); LIMITED/no authority → court confirmation + appraisal + overbid auction required.
- 95% of probate cases are handled by attorneys who've done one probate or less in the last two years — most attorneys are NOT probate experts and create the delays that create "stuck probates."
- Probate timeline when properly managed: ~2-6 months to sale-readiness; litigated cases 2+ years.
- Occasional flips: ~2/year, ~$400-420K purchases, ~$50K combined net — investing is a side dish, brokerage is the meal.

## What He Argues Against
- Chasing "fresh" probate filings with one mailer — the money is in sustained multi-year presence and in the STUCK cases everyone else abandoned.
- Blind attorney referrals: "Attorneys as a general rule sound great when they know nothing." For simple estates he openly recommends DIY/flat-fee services (e.g., EZ-Probate ~$654 DIY / ~$1,250 concierge) over $10,000+ conventional attorneys: "they do a better job with the paperwork than most attorneys do"; "80 or 90% of cases in LA county don't need an attorney."
- Days-on-market worship: "Days on market has more to do with realtors" (marketing choices) than with market demand.
- Off-market/pre-marketing games: "it helps the agent... it doesn't help the customer for a dollar."
- Buy-and-hold in high-cost LA today: "I don't believe I would do it today in Los Angeles County."

## Mistakes He Calls Out
- Agents learning probate law to impress instead of building a specialist Rolodex: "don't learn it to be the expert, know enough to find the expert" (eviction attorneys, probate filing services, estate-planning platforms).
- Waiting for perfect conditions: new agents shouldn't even wait for a license — "I would put together a plan for an investor marketing approach" immediately.
- Treating heirs like leads instead of families needing weekly status updates.
