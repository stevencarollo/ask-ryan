# Chad Corbett — Probate Systems & Family-First Outreach (Researched Dossier)

## Philosophy
- Co-founded All The Leads (sold shares 2020), created Probate Mastery / Estate Professionals Mastermind. Course is "designed largely around psychology of people in situations" — attract, engage, build rapport, "do the right things in the right order."
- Lead with value, magnetize: "If you lead with value and be empathetic to their situation, this business just comes to you like a magnet."
- Reputation above revenue: "Your reputation is everything, and I've never been willing to risk it over a damn paycheck."
- The Swiss-army-knife positioning: a solution for everything the estate throws up — trusted advisor managing the estate's whole financial resolution, "not just a home-sale facilitator."

## Named Frameworks
- **Monetize every conversation** — his USP: even when the house already sold, pivot to (a) private-money lending conversations (average US inheritance ≈ $77K), (b) investment purchases (duplex/triplex), (c) trust & estate-planning referrals, (d) insurance/investment strategy referrals. Pivot script: "Do you have a plan for investing those proceeds, or will you spend the inheritance like most people? That would essentially erase your loved one's legacy."
- **The 68-day rule** — median gap from death to probate FILING is ~68 days (nationwide data). By filing time families have processed early grief and are more logical; bring confident, positive energy — do NOT match their emotional low.
- **Pre-probate = INBOUND ONLY**: "I do not believe in direct marketing, in outbound direct mail and cold calling these lists" (pre-probate). Skip-trace error rates ~40% — you risk calling minors or the wrong family member. Instead: community Facebook groups as safe environments; skip-traced lists used only as Facebook custom audiences.
- **Market-based pricing with automatic reductions** (for the emotional overpriced estate listing): list at the family's price, but write scheduled reductions into the listing agreement (every 7-14 days, actual dates in additional terms). Script: "I genuinely hope we achieve your price, but based on comps we'll need strategic adjustment. Let's commit to scheduled reductions so we never become a stagnant listing."
- **Digital door-knocking** — reach vendors/attorneys via mutual Facebook connections and soft intros rather than cold pitches.
- **Attorney partnership positioning** — never lead with "realtor": "I have a social enterprise here in the community that helps families... I want to connect with attorneys whose values align with mine." Value prop: take the "redundant non-dollar-productive aspects of a case" (the emotional, non-billable support work) off the attorney's desk. Flip the frame — YOU interview THEM: "I'd like to interview you to see if you would qualify to represent my client." Scale truth: "Two or three of these relationships can be a lifetime of fulfilling business."
- **Vendor team engine** — probate attorneys (primary), CPAs/RIAs, trust advisors, estate-sale companies, senior-living directors of sales (commissioned = motivated), funeral directors. Choose partners with a servant mentality.

## Sales Language (verbatim techniques)
- Never "Are you thinking about selling?" — too direct. Instead create imagined experience: "Where would you put the sofa?"
- Kill "why" questions (defensive trigger) → use "How important is...?"
- Autopilot-objection breaker: "That's the famous words of 9 out of 10 of my best clients."
- Don't open cold calls with the agent title — credibility via specifics instead: "I met with the probate court clerk this week," or a named professional referral.

## Numbers
- ~$77K average US inheritance; ~77% of inherited properties are FREE AND CLEAR.
- Direct-mail probate campaigns: ~2% response target on proven letters; 83% of closings happen between touches 7-12; competition drops off after month 3-4 — commit to 12+ months; families who delayed 6+ months are the highest-need prospects.
- Probate cost framing for estate-planning referrals: probate ≈ 4-8% of gross estate value vs the much cheaper trust setup.

## What He Argues Against
- Cold-calling/mailing pre-probate lists (reputation risk + 40% data error).
- Matching the family's grief energy on calls (confident positivity serves better by filing time).
- One-trick agents who only know "list it" — leaving 3 of 4 monetization paths on the table.
- Transactional partner-picking: "If they're that kind of person... you're going to get screwed by him too."

## Business Planning
- Annual Napoleon Hill 28-question self-analysis; quarterly milestones over annual goals; written 5-year plan with a clear why; "measure the gain, not the gap." Reading list he pushes: Start with NO (Jim Camp), The Gap and The Gain (Sullivan), Think and Grow Rich (Hill).
