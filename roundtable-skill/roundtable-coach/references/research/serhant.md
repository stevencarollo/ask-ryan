# Ryan Serhant — Luxury Sales & Personal Branding (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- CEO/founder of SERHANT. (brokerage + media company); star of Million Dollar Listing NY and Owning Manhattan; author of "Sell It Like Serhant," "Big Money Energy," and "Brand It Like Serhant"; built one of NYC's top sales teams before launching his own brokerage on a content-first model.

## Named Frameworks (from "Sell It Like Serhant" — researched)
- **The Three F's**: Follow-up (send it IMMEDIATELY after connecting — never sit and wait), Follow-through (if you said you'd do it, do it; keep clients updated through the whole process), Follow-back (reconnect with past clients on a rhythm).
- **Finder / Keeper / Doer**: allocate time across three roles daily regardless of workload — Finder (new business/planning), Keeper (finance/strategy/legal), Doer (execution). Most agents are all-Doer and starve their pipeline.
- **Ball juggling**: "Keep more balls in the air" — work many deals simultaneously; hyper-focusing on one deal creates desperation and kills optionality.
- **Six Reasons Balls Drop** (deal-killers): (1) failure to communicate with clients, (2) REPLYING instead of responding (rushed one-line answers vs thoughtful responses), (3) setting unrealistic expectations, (4) lacking required knowledge, (5) wrong approach for that specific client, (6) focusing on the money instead of the client.
- **The Four W's** (his work tenets): WHY (purpose behind the goal), WORK (the actions), WALL (what you're running FROM — leverage the fear), WIN (what success means to you).
- **The Seven Client Stages** (emotional arc of every deal): Excitement → Frustration → Fear → Disappointment → Acceptance → Happiness → Relief. Manage the STAGE, not just the transaction — most blowups are an agent treating a Fear-stage client with Excitement-stage energy.
- **The Wow Effect** (anchoring): present the premium option FIRST to anchor expectations, then the middle option closes.
- **"Build the house first"**: essentials before decorations — clients and revenue before office, logo, and vanity spend.
- **Daily connection quota**: "You are surrounded by opportunity" — 3+ new contacts every day from anywhere (elevator, gym, showing).
- **Day-before planning**: "Your day should start a day before" — plan the night prior, execute strictly, hardest task first while energy is highest.

## Sales Psychology (verbatim-anchored)
- **"People don't like being sold. They like to shop with friends."** — the single most-quoted Serhant line; be the friend with expertise, not the closer.
- People buy emotionally, not rationally — sell the improved LIFE, not the product (lifestyle, not square footage).
- Objections appear in nearly every deal — expect them, agree first, reframe second, never defend.
- Face-to-face dramatically improves close rates; say YES to uncomfortable opportunities (his career started with a yes to an open house no one wanted).

## Brand Doctrine (SERHANT. era)
- Consistency, authenticity, visibility, value — the brand tenets; video before you're comfortable; hook-first short-form (first seconds decide); the personal brand is the pipeline that never cold-calls.

## What He Argues Against
- Waiting for the phone to ring; single-deal dependence; replying instead of responding; money-first energy (clients smell it); perfection before publishing content.
