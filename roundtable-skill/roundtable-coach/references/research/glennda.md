# Glennda Baker — Storytelling & Social Media (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Atlanta/Marietta agent with 26+ years in the business; TikTok's most recognizable real estate storyteller: joined October 2020 with 122 followers → **565,000 in one year**; videos regularly pull 30,000 to 2 million likes; **$241,000 GCI directly from TikTok leads in 2021**; one two-day stretch produced two relocation buyers + three agent referrals.

## Content Philosophy (verbatim)
- "People want to see what they don't know. That's why they tune in to me. It's like talking to a friend. They're coming to me for the truth and the real life of real estate."
- Her #1 advice to agents: **"Be you — and just turn up the volume."**
- Three success factors (her list): (1) consistency in messaging, (2) regular posting schedule, (3) active audience engagement — "If you do these three things, you will build your own following."

## The Method (verified mechanics)
- **Interview-style storytelling**: videos framed as if the viewer is overhearing a conversation with a friend — not presentations, not listings tours. Anecdotes from 26 years: the time she sold 27 houses via Match.com, blowing a commission check, the $137K single sale — REAL numbers, real mistakes, real consequences.
- **Story formula**: specific detail + emotional stake + real-estate lesson. Cautionary tales and "what agents won't tell you" insider truths outperform fancy house pictures — "your TikTok posts don't have to be just showing off fancy house pictures."
- **Batch production**: records 30+ videos in a single monthly session — consistency is manufactured in batches, not willpower.
- **The morning ritual**: wakes 5:50-6:10 and posts a TikTok before getting out of bed — daily posting so reliable that followers message her when she misses a day.
- **Brand signatures**: unfiltered Southern accent, tell-it-like-it-is delivery, star-covered wardrobe matching her catchphrase "Oh, my stars!" — deliberate, ownable identity markers.

## What She Argues Against
- Polished listing-photo content (nobody tunes in for it).
- Copying other creators instead of amplifying yourself.
- Posting sporadically and expecting the algorithm to care.
- Hiding mistakes — her losses ARE the content that converts trust.
