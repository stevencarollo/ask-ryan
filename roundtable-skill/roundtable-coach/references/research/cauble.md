# Tyler Cauble — Commercial Leasing & Small CRE (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Founder of The Cauble Group (Nashville boutique commercial brokerage — office, retail, industrial); CEO of Hamilton Development (**2.1M+ sq ft acquired since 2019**); author of "Open for Business: The Insider's Guide to Leasing Commercial Real Estate"; major CRE-education YouTube/podcast presence; self-paced courses aimed at INVESTORS and principals (not agents).

## Cap Rate Teaching (verified, with his numbers)
- Formula: **Cap Rate = NOI ÷ Property Value.** "A $1,000,000 building generating $80,000 in NOI is an 8% cap rate."
- Doctrine: "Cap rate is a SNAPSHOT, not a return on investment." And: "A 7-cap industrial deal and a 7-cap retail deal aren't the same thing."
- His 2026 ranges by asset class: Multifamily Class A 4.5-6.0% (lowest yield, most liquid); Industrial big-box 5.5-7.0% (credit-tenant leases); CBD Class A office 6.0-8.0% (post-WFH repricing); Class B office 8.5-11% (highest yield, most risk); anchored retail 6.5-8.0%; investment-grade net lease 5.0-6.5%; retail strips 7.0-9.0%.
- Reading the bands: 5-cap = trophy; 6 = solid Class A/anchor in growth markets; 7 = market rate for stabilized; 8+ = real risk OR value-add requiring active management.
- "Cap rate is one of three or four metrics you should be running on every deal. It's not the most important one for most investors" — pair with cash-on-cash and IRR.

## Lease Literacy (his core beginner teaching)
- Lease types change the ECONOMICS of a deal: **NNN** (tenant pays taxes, insurance, CAM — "the backbone of single-tenant retail and a lot of multi-tenant shopping centers... usually sold to investors as 'mailbox money'"), **modified gross**, and **full-service gross**. Underwrite the lease, not just the building.

## Four Investor Mistakes He Calls Out (verified list)
1. **Pro forma over actuals** — accepting broker "stabilized" numbers instead of trailing-twelve-month ACTUAL NOI.
2. **Cross-asset cap comparison** — ignoring tenant credit, lease length, and capex profiles.
3. **Ignoring NOI growth trajectory** — entry cap rate obsession while missing where income goes.
4. **Confusing metrics** — cap rate (unleveraged) vs cash-on-cash (leveraged) are different animals.

## Small-CRE Doctrine
- Commercial demystified for regular investors: start neighborhood-scale (small retail strips, mixed-use, small office) where institutions don't compete; value-add levers = below-market rents rolled at renewal, lease-up, NNN conversion, expense recapture. Commercial financing differs fundamentally from home mortgages — teach the debt before the deal.
