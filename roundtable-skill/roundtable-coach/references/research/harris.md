## Core Doctrine
- LISTINGS are the business — the whole system is engineered to "locate & prequalify motivated sellers" and "get the listing every single time" via their proprietary stack: Listing Evaluation → Pre-Listing Package → Listing Presentation.
- **The 7 P's** (their profit formula, verbatim): "The profit comes from Previewing, Prequalifying, Preparing, the PLP [Pre-Listing Package], and a Polished, Professional Presentation."
- The 7-step listing machine: generate the lead → follow up → prequalify → preview → prepare/PLP → present → close/sign. Skipping prequalification is the cardinal sin.
- Lead-gen rule: run a MINIMUM of 5 "spokes" of lead generation simultaneously, and "do more prospecting than you think necessary."

## The Buyer Pre-Qualification Script (verbatim, researched)
Five questions, in order:
1. **Exclusivity**: "I assume because you're calling me directly, you're not already working with another agent?" — commitment check before any effort.
2. **Financial**: "What price has your lender told you NOT to go above?" — one question surfaces cash/pre-approved/no-lender instantly.
3. **Hidden listing lead**: "Which home in the neighborhood do you plan on selling?" — their emphasized insight: "Many buyer leads are listing leads in disguise."
4. **Needs**: "Paint me a picture. Ideally, what are you looking for in your next home?"
5. **Commitment scale**: "On a scale of 1-10, where would you rate your desire to write up an offer?" — and if not a 10: "What would it take to make you a 10?"

## Seller Prequalification (their public framework)
- Prequalify EVERY listing appointment before going: motivation (why/where/when), timeline, price expectations, all decision-makers present, and competition (are they interviewing other agents?).
