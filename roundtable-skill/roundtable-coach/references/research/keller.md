# Gary Keller — Team Building & Wealth Models / MREA (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Co-founder of Keller Williams (the largest real estate franchise by agent count); author of "The Millionaire Real Estate Agent" (with Dave Jenks & Jay Papasan) — the industry's most-cited business book — plus "The ONE Thing," "SHIFT," and "The Millionaire Real Estate Investor."

## The Three L's (verbatim-anchored)
1. **Leads**: "Until you have enough leads (to meet or exceed your goals), there is no other issue."
2. **Listings**: "Listings are the high-leverage, maximum earning opportunity in our industry."
3. **Leverage**: people, systems, tools — the who/how/what of scaling beyond yourself.

## The Four MREA Models
- **Economic Model**: Appointments × conversion rate = listings → × conversion = sales → × average price = volume → × commission = gross revenue − expenses = net income. APPOINTMENTS are the master KPI — know exactly how many appointments your income goal requires and work backward to daily conversations.
- **Lead Generation Model** — the researched touch systems:
  - **8x8**: one touch per week for 8 weeks to embed a NEW contact (typical sequence: digital message, mail, mail, phone call, free report, home-upkeep tip, giveaway, closing phone call).
  - **33 Touch**: the annual nurture after 8x8 — 18 emails/mailings/letters + 8 thank-you/thinking-of-you cards + 3 calls + 2 birthday cards + Mother's Day + Father's Day = 33 systematic touches/year.
  - Doctrine: "It's a numbers game. The more you bump up your numbers, the more success you will see." Database size × touch discipline = predictable closings.
- **Budget Model**: lead with revenue, budget conservatively — "red light, green light": keep funding what returns more than it costs, stop what doesn't; lead generation is the #1 budget line; hold profit discipline.
- **Organizational Model**: FIRST HIRE IS ADMINISTRATIVE — systematize operations before adding salespeople; then buyer specialists, then listing specialists. Hire TALENT over cheap help every time (talent = the 20% who produce 80%).

## The 8 Goal Categories (his tracking stack)
Leads generated · Listings · Contracts written · Contracts closed · Money · People · Systems/Tools · Personal education.

## ONE Thing Doctrine (companion book)
- The focusing question: "What's the ONE thing I can do such that by doing it everything else becomes easier or unnecessary?" Time-block lead generation like a paid appointment; until the ONE thing is done, everything else is a distraction.

## Wealth Track
- Agent → business owner → investor: convert active commission income into holdings; the 7th-level business runs without you. Think in models and decades, not transactions.

## What He Argues Against
- Creative improvisation before mastering proven models ("models first, creativity second").
- Hiring salespeople before admin leverage.
- Cost-cutting your way to profit instead of revenue-leading.
- Treating lead generation as optional when busy — it IS the job.
