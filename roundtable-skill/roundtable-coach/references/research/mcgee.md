# Shea McGee — Design Rules & Transformations (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Co-founder (with Syd McGee) of **Studio McGee** and McGee & Co.; star of Netflix's "Dream Home Makeover"; Target's Threshold-designed-with-Studio-McGee mass lines; co-author of "Make Life Beautiful." Company mission (verbatim): **"Make Life Beautiful for ourselves and those around us."**

## Philosophy
- A home should be "both beautiful and deeply personal" — projects are "not a showcase of trends but a reflection of the people who live there."
- Scalability doctrine: "a good set of core design values can be applied to any home, even with the smallest of footprints" — the same principles from estate homes apply at every size and budget.

## Design Rules (her researched framework)
- **Scale and proportion first** — the most common failure point; size UP: rugs that anchor the furniture, art and lighting proportionate to the wall and room. Getting scale right is what makes rooms read "designed."
- **Layer textures for timelessness**: neutral palettes gain depth through texture — wood, metal, fabrics combined for "a rich, tactile experience"; "layering materials will help to create a timeless scheme that feels curated and cozy."
- **Break the symmetry**: nailing a furniture scheme is "a delicate balance between symmetry, asymmetry, and finally ditching matching sets" — perfect symmetry reads flat; the deliberate break makes it feel collected.
- **Design to age**: her Arizona new-build was designed "to look better in 20 years than it does today" — write the history INTO a new house (materials that patina, vintage anchors) instead of letting it feel like a builder-grade time capsule.

## The 4 Small-Space Signature Tips (verified list)
1. **Furniture with subtle details** — refined understated embellishments over bold statement pieces; sophistication without visual overwhelm.
2. **Vintage accents — always** — character, depth, and a curated quality at any square footage.
3. **Layer up the textures** — richness and dimension even in limited rooms.
4. **Add a romantic flourish** — the soft final touch that creates her "laid-back luxury."

## Application for Agents (The Roundtable's use)
- Listing prep sequencing on a budget: scale-check the furniture (undersized rugs/art scream cheap) → layer textiles → one vintage anchor per key room → styled-but-lived-in for photos. Rooms styled by these rules photograph "designed" without renovation dollars.
- The before/after mindset: her brand is TRANSFORMATION — show buyers and sellers the delta, not the decor.

## What She Argues Against
- Matching furniture sets (the #1 amateur tell).
- Undersized rugs, art, and lighting.
- Trend-chasing over timeless layering.
- Flat single-texture neutral rooms ("neutral" fails without texture depth).
