# Chelsea Peitz — Visual Social Branding (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Full-time Realtor for a decade+ before moving to marketing/social coaching (2011); author of **"Talking in Pictures"** (the first book on camera-first personal branding) and **"What to Post: How to Create Engaging Social Media Content that Builds Your Brand and Gets Results (for Real Estate)"**; national keynote speaker (KW Events, industry conferences); **Inman's Most Influential Marketers in Residential Real Estate 2023** and **Inman 2024 Marketing All-Stars**.

## Core Philosophy (verbatim-anchored)
- **"The ONLY original content is your face, your voice, lived experience, perspective, opinion and story — without that, you only have content, not CONTEXT."** The differentiator isn't the market update; it's that YOU delivered it.
- Camera-first doctrine: camera-first platforms changed personal branding forever — "Talking in Pictures" was inspired by Snapchat CEO Evan Spiegel's insight that kids taking photos all day "aren't preserving memories, they're TALKING." The camera is a communication channel, not a production studio.

## The Method (her teaching stack)
- **Show your face** — people hire the face they see weekly; face on camera early in the video, eye contact with the lens, captions always (most watch muted).
- **Document, don't create** — the day you're already living IS the content: the inspection, the key handoff, the pricing conversation (anonymized). Stop inventing; start narrating.
- **Stories > posts for trust** — daily, casual, interactive (stickers/polls/questions); feed posts for authority; the combination builds the know-like-trust ladder: personality content (KNOW) → values/process content (LIKE) → proof content: testimonials, closings, market calls (TRUST).
- **"The money is in the DMs"** — engagement psychology: reply to every comment fast, move conversations to DMs — DM conversations convert; feeds don't.
- **Search-optimized social** — captions with keywords and location tags now that social platforms ARE search engines.
- **No dancing required** — teach one thing, tell one story, or take one stand per post; brand-building for grown professionals.

## What She Argues Against
- Faceless brand accounts and logo-first "branding."
- Perfection paralysis — connection beats production value.
- Posting without engaging (the feed is the billboard; the DM is the closing table).
- Copying trends that don't fit your voice ("show up like the incredible human you are").
