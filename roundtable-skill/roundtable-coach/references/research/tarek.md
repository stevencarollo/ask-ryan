## Credentials
- Flipped close to **1,000 properties**, sold 1,000+ houses; 200+ personal rentals; HGTV's Flip or Flop, Flipping 101, The Flipping El Moussas; author of "Flip Your Life" (2024); ventures span wholesaling, fix-and-flip, private equity, production, education, and solar.

## The Four-Step Process (from "Flip Your Life")
**Evaluate → Emulate → Renovate → Duplicate**: evaluate the situation honestly (property or life), emulate people who've already done it, renovate deliberately (fix foundations before cosmetics), duplicate what works into a repeatable system.

## Flip Craft (researched)
- **ARV discipline**: calculate After Repair Value from neighborhood comps + current market trends + projected repair costs — before the offer, always. House rule (Heather's articulation on their shows): **"Renovate to what the comparables support, not what you think will sell."** Over-improving past the comp band is donated money.
- **Budget doctrine**: spend where value returns — kitchens and baths carry the appraisal; their specific tip: "Always include at least one bathtub to appeal to a broad range of buyers" (families need one).
- **Cash offers** streamline negotiations and win deals — certainty beats price in distressed acquisitions.
- **Foundations before cosmetics** — structural/mechanical issues solved before pretty finishes (both a flip rule and the book's life metaphor).
- Budget reality (Flipping 101 recurring theme): piecemealed budgets kill novices — scope and price the WHOLE project before demo day; every day of holding costs money.

## Mindset (his through-line)
- Persistence through distress is the brand: cancer (thyroid + testicular), back injury, divorce, bankruptcy-adjacent early years — "Keep going. We all have to keep going no matter what life throws in our paths."
- Build your "crew" carefully — choose team members on moral character, not just price.
- "Find opportunity in distress" — distressed properties, distressed markets, distressed moments are where the margin lives.

## What He Argues Against
- Renovating to personal taste instead of the comp band.
- Skipping the ARV math because a deal "feels" good.
- Piecemeal budgeting mid-project.
- Cosmetic lipstick over structural problems.
- Quitting after a setback deal (the 1,000 flips include losers — the system survives them).
