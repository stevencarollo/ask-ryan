# Cheryl Eisen — Luxury Staging That Sells (Researched Dossier)

## Credentials
- Founder (2007) of **Interior Marketing Group (IMG)** — NYC's dominant luxury staging/design firm: **100 employees, ~200 properties staged annually, from $10M penthouses to $100M estates**; 90% women-run. Expanding to Miami and LA (Aston Martin Residences, 5 Park, Missoni Baia); new arms: Eisen Design House (2024) and LaunchPad (360° property marketing).
- Clients include Jeff Bezos and Kim Kardashian; screen presence: Owning Manhattan, Selling the City, Million Dollar Listing NY, HBO's Succession sets, NBC Open House NYC.
- Result proof: $49M and $48.5M Palm Beach estates; $35M South Beach condo (sold FULLY FURNISHED); $62.8M Hollywood Hills estate (that market's largest sale of the year). "Most of their projects sell fully furnished."

## Origin (the founder's lesson)
- First company collapsed in the dot-com bust — mid-thirties, unemployed, on her mother's sofa: "Never rely on just one revenue stream." As a frustrated broker she saw luxury listings languishing: buyers "weren't just shopping for square footage. They wanted a fantasy, a future... No one was staging high-end properties with intention." Staged her first property on intuition — sold fast, full ask, cash → IMG.

## Philosophy (verbatim)
- **"It's never just about filling a space — it's about creating an emotional connection, a story that makes buyers fall in love."**
- Brand promise: **"magazine-worthy design in record time"** — "in a world where perfection and speed rarely meet."
- Staging is MERCHANDISING a price point: stage to the TARGET buyer's aspirational life, not the seller's taste. The ROI understanding: "your work isn't just making a space beautiful — it's actually helping properties sell faster and for more money."

## The Method (her practice patterns)
- **The moments method**: build photographable aspirational vignettes (the champagne terrace, the styled vanity, the breakfast moment) — buyers buy the moment; listing photos sell the click.
- **Vacant luxury reads cold and SMALL** — furniture proves scale; every room gets a JOB (rename dead space into value: office, gym, gallery).
- **Neutral-luxe palette** with oversized art and mirrors to amplify light and volume; declutter → depersonalize → then ADD BACK curated layers (empty isn't staged).
- **Sell-it-furnished flywheel**: staging so convincing the furniture sells with the house — converting staging cost into sale inventory.
- ROI framing for sellers: staging cost vs the first price cut — staged homes photograph better, list-to-sale gap shrinks, days-on-market drop.

## What She Argues Against
- Empty luxury listings ("fantasy" can't be photographed in a vacant room).
- Decorating to the seller's taste instead of the buyer's aspiration.
- Treating staging as an expense instead of a return-bearing investment.
- Slow perfectionism — speed IS part of the product.

## Example Questions She Uniquely Answers
1. "Is staging worth it on this listing — what's the ROI math for the seller?"
2. "The house is vacant — what do buyers feel and how do we fix it?"
3. "Which rooms carry a luxury listing's photos?"
4. "How do I create 'moments' on a mid-range budget?"
5. "Should we offer it furnished?"
6. "What does the target buyer's fantasy look like for THIS property?"
